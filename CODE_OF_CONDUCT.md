# Code of Conduct

> **AZMX AI — The sovereign agent platform.**

AZMX is a place where people who work on, with, or around the platform should be able to participate without being harassed, dismissed, or made to feel unsafe.

## The short version

- **Be respectful.** Disagree with the work, not the person.
- **Be patient.** Everyone is at a different point on the learning curve.
- **Be honest.** Don't claim work you didn't do, don't hide what isn't working, don't post secrets in public.
- **Be inclusive.** Contributors and users span many time zones, languages, ages, and backgrounds.

## What's not OK

- Personal attacks, harassment, sustained disruption, doxxing, or threats.
- Sexualized language or imagery, unwelcome sexual attention.
- Discriminatory jokes or language — racial, ethnic, gender, sexual orientation, religion, disability, age, body.
- Posting other people's private information (email, phone, address, IP, employer, ID).
- Posting working API keys, credentials, or other secrets — even your own.
- Spam or off-topic promotion.

## Where this applies

Anywhere AZMX exists publicly: GitHub issues, PRs, Discussions, code comments, commit messages, any official chat space, and any event where someone is representing the project.

## Reporting

Email **security@azmx.app** (it's our default private channel until a dedicated one exists). Include enough detail to act on. Reports are read by a small group; we acknowledge within 24 hours and act within a week unless we tell you otherwise.

You will not be retaliated against for reporting in good faith — including reporting about a maintainer.

## Consequences

Maintainers may, at their discretion: remove a comment, lock a thread, warn a participant, temporarily restrict, or permanently ban. We will explain our reasoning where it's safe to do so.

## Inspiration

This document borrows the structure of [Contributor Covenant v2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/) but is written in our own voice. If anything is unclear, ask.
